Reflections on Technology Dependency

So much of it has passed by addiction and become a social, national, and global dependency.

1.1: Technology is very common place now. Any of us would be hard pressed to define an addiction that doesn't include our technology practices at school or in the work place. Is it an addiction that I can take my time on the crapper and simultaneously move money, accomplish work and school needs, or just have a good healthy laugh. Which, brings up another point. Technology is so personal and unique in the experience it is providing for individuals. How do we know whose on their phone more or who of our family and friends are over invested in a technical toxicology? We are all depending on technology all the time.

1.2: If its about health and safety, then collectively as a consumer demographic we need to turn to the manufacturers and demand that they consider our mental and physical health more wisely as they create and engineer future technology. The lights on cars have become entirely all to bright. The LED displays on the sides of the highways at night are clear and present danger. I mean, what about the commercial industries addiction to spend billions of dollars, toxically access earth resources, and enslave populations in order to provide the disposable technology industry that has become common place?

1.3: Where do we draw the line at addiction? Bags under your eyes? Pain in your lumbar? Depression? anxiety? Or maybe slavery? Pollution of the waters? Or maybe pollution of the future minds as tools of technology instead of users? How do we interactively stop it from hurting us and keep assisting it in helping us?
