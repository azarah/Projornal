1. Strive for Consistency

Consistency in design elements and interactions across the interface helps users build mental models and navigate the system more easily.

2. Seek Universal Usability

Universal usability encourages designers to provide alternative means of interaction and content presentation to accommodate all users.

Example of Violation

3. Offer Informative Feedback

The interface should provide clear and timely feedback to users about the outcome of their actions.

Example of Violation

4. Design Dialogs to Yield Closure

Dialog boxes and pop-ups should lead to clear and decisive actions.

Example of Violation

5. Offer Simple Error Handling

Error messages should be clear, precise, and suggest how to correct the issue.

Example of Violation

6. Permit Easy Reversal of Actions

Users should be able to undo their actions and easily recover from mistakes.

Example of Violation

7. Keep Users in Control

Users should feel in control of their interactions with the interface.

8. Reduce Short-Term Memory Load

Minimize the cognitive load on users by presenting information and choices in a way that doesn't overwhelm their working memory.
