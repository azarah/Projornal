Comparing Design Interfaces

Radio Buttons (RB)

Pros:

  • Visibility: All options are visible at once, allowing quick scanning and selection.
  • Speed: Faster selection since all options are immediately visible and accessible with a single click.
  • Simplicity: Presents a straightforward choice for a small number of options.

Cons:

  • Space Usage: Takes up more space on the interface.
  • Broken Site Line: The site line may be broken, affecting selection clarity for some users.

Performance:

Users can immediately see all options, allowing for quick recognition and selection with minimal interaction.

Combo-Box (CB)

Pros:

  • Space Efficiency: Saves screen space by showing options in a drop-down menu.
  • Scalability: Easier to accommodate a larger number of options without overwhelming the user.

Cons:

  • Interaction Required: Users must click to open the drop-down list, adding an extra step.
  • Visibility: Options are not immediately visible, which may delay selection.

Performance:

Introduces additional clicks and requires scanning of the drop-down list, adding steps to the selection process.

Participatory Design Process

  • Identify Participants: Select a diverse group of elderly users.
  • Initial Workshop: Introduce the proposed designs and gather first impressions.
  • Education Session: Offer a brief tutorial on the current designs.
  • Feedback Rounds: Engage participants in hands-on sessions and collect feedback.
  • Co-Design Sessions: Invite participants to suggest modifications or alternative designs.
  • Prototype Testing: Develop a prototype incorporating the feedback.
  • Iterative Feedback and Refinement: Use iterative cycles to fine-tune the design.
  • Final Review: Conduct a final review session to ensure all concerns are addressed.

Social Impact Statement Process

  • Assessment: Collect data on potential users' demographics and preferences.
  • Engagement: Engage with the community for insights on challenges and design context.
  • Drafting the Statement: Outline the intended benefits of the redesigned interface.
  • Public Review: Invite feedback from a broader community and experts.
  • Implementation and Monitoring: Implement design changes and monitor impact over time.
  • Reporting: Write a final assessment of the design process and observed impacts.

Design an Experiment

Objective

To evaluate the effectiveness of a larger font size and a numbered list to improve interface usability for elderly and motor-impaired users.

Hypothesis

The new design with larger font size and a numbered list will significantly improve usability for elderly and motor-impaired users.

Participants

  • Elderly users aged 65 and above.
  • Users with varying degrees of motor impairment.
  • A control group of users without motor impairments.

Methodology

  1. Pre-experiment Survey: Collect baseline data on demographics and computer proficiency.
  2. Training Session: Provide a brief training session on both designs.
  3. Task Design: Create tasks reflecting real-world scenarios for both designs.
  4. Experiment Setup: Use a within-subjects design with randomization.
  5. Data Collection: Measure task completion time, accuracy, and collect qualitative feedback.
  6. Analysis: Perform statistical analyses to compare performance and satisfaction.
  7. Post-Experiment Review: Conduct a debriefing session to gather additional insights.

Expected Outcomes

The experiment aims to provide empirical evidence on whether the new design improves usability for elderly and motor-impaired users.

Participatory Design

Participatory design involves all stakeholders in the design process to ensure the result meets their needs and is usable.

Benefits

  • Enhanced outcomes through diverse perspectives.
  • Increased adoption due to stakeholder involvement.
  • Innovation from diverse ideas.
  • Social equity by addressing underserved groups' needs.

Challenges

  • Resource-intensive process.
  • Managing differing opinions and expectations.
  • Ensuring equal representation of all stakeholder groups.

Objectives and Scope

  • Develop prototypes and validate concepts with users.

Document and Communicate Findings
